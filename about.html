About Asphodel Park

Asphodel Park opened its gates in 1932 on the wooded outskirts near what is now Yellow Creek State Park in Indiana County, Pennsylvania. Its origins trace back to 1924, when business partners Felix Mortimer, Ethan Sinclair, and Jasper Monroe purchased and restored a struggling performance venue known as The Shattered Hall. The theater quickly became a proving ground for ambitious live shows, traveling exhibitions, and experimental attractions.

Over the following decades, Asphodel Park expanded at a pace that surprised even its contemporaries. At its height, the park featured an unusual scale of rides, stages, and themed areas, earning a reputation for spectacle and excess. Records from the period are inconsistent, however, and contemporary accounts often disagree on layouts, attractions, and even attendance figures.

At some point in the late 20th century, Asphodel Park closed without formal announcement. Official documentation is sparse, and the circumstances surrounding its disappearance remain unclear. What is known is that the land was later found cleared and undeveloped, with no remaining structures. Today, Asphodel Park does not appear on most historical maps, and references to its founders are limited to fragmented archives and secondhand recollections.
